# Linguistics

The scientific study of language — its sound, structure, meaning, use, acquisition, and
change. This folder is the college-level core, and it is the field
[large language models](../ai/large-language-models.md) are built to model: subword
[tokenization](morphology.md) echoes morphology, [self-attention](../ai/transformers-and-attention.md)
learns [syntactic](syntax.md) dependencies, and word [embeddings](../ai/representation-learning-and-embeddings.md)
are [distributional semantics](semantics.md) made computational. LLMs also reopened old
debates — nativism vs statistical learning — from the data side (see
[language acquisition](language-acquisition.md), [universal grammar](universal-grammar.md)).

## The shape of the field

Language is studied in layers: [phonetics & phonology](phonetics-and-phonology.md) (sound),
[morphology](morphology.md) (word structure), [syntax](syntax.md) (sentence structure),
[semantics](semantics.md) (meaning), and [pragmatics](pragmatics.md) (meaning in context).
Around that core sit [language acquisition](language-acquisition.md) (how we learn it),
[psycholinguistics](psycholinguistics.md) (how we process it),
[sociolinguistics](sociolinguistics.md) (how it varies in society),
[historical linguistics](historical-linguistics.md) (how it changes), and the
[universal grammar](universal-grammar.md) debate about what is innate.
[Computational linguistics & NLP](computational-linguistics-and-nlp.md) is the bridge to
[AI](../ai/index.md).

## Concepts

- [Phonetics and Phonology](phonetics-and-phonology.md) — speech sounds (articulation, acoustics, IPA) vs the mind's sound system (phonemes, rules)
- [Morphology](morphology.md) — word structure: morphemes, inflection vs derivation; the ancestor of subword tokenization
- [Syntax](syntax.md) — hierarchical sentence structure: constituents, phrase-structure trees, generative grammar, recursion
- [Semantics](semantics.md) — meaning: compositionality, truth conditions, sense vs reference, distributional semantics
- [Pragmatics](pragmatics.md) — meaning in context: speech acts, Grice's maxims and implicature, deixis
- [Language Acquisition](language-acquisition.md) — how children learn language; poverty of the stimulus; what LLMs learn from data
- [Psycholinguistics](psycholinguistics.md) — language in the mind: parsing, the mental lexicon, garden-path sentences, surprisal
- [Sociolinguistics](sociolinguistics.md) — structured variation, dialect/register, prestige, code-switching, social bias in data
- [Historical Linguistics](historical-linguistics.md) — sound change, the comparative method, language families, grammaticalization
- [Universal Grammar](universal-grammar.md) — Chomsky's innate language faculty, universals and typology, and the contrast with data-driven LLMs
- [Computational Linguistics and NLP](computational-linguistics-and-nlp.md) — from formal grammars through the statistical turn to transformers and LLMs

## Canonical works

- [An Introduction to Language](fromkin-introduction-to-language.md) — Fromkin, Rodman & Hyams; the standard survey
- [Syntactic Structures](chomsky-syntactic-structures.md) — Chomsky; the book that launched generative grammar
- [Speech and Language Processing](jurafsky-martin-speech-and-language-processing.md) — Jurafsky & Martin; the NLP bible (free)
- [Course in General Linguistics](saussure-course-in-general-linguistics.md) — Saussure; founding structuralism
- [The Language Instinct](pinker-language-instinct.md) — Pinker; language as an evolved biological instinct
