# Sociology

The study of social life — how human behavior is shaped by groups, institutions, and
structures, and how those in turn are made and remade by people. This folder is the
college-level core. It connects across HAL: [organizations & bureaucracy](organizations-and-bureaucracy.md)
underlies the org side of [AI adoption](../ai-org/index.md);
[social networks & capital](social-networks-and-capital.md) is the sociology behind
[network science](../systems-thinking/network-science.md) and
[network effects](../economics/information-economics-and-network-effects.md); and
[technology & society](technology-and-society.md) frames AI's social impact alongside
[AI governance](../ai-governance/index.md).

## The shape of the field

[Sociological theory](sociological-theory.md) gives the three classical lenses —
functionalism, conflict theory, symbolic interactionism — and the founders (Durkheim,
Marx, Weber). Its central tension is [structure and agency](social-structure-and-agency.md).
Society is transmitted through [culture and socialization](culture-and-socialization.md) and
organized by [social institutions](social-institutions.md) and
[organizations & bureaucracy](organizations-and-bureaucracy.md). It is unequal —
[stratification & inequality](social-stratification-and-inequality.md),
[race, gender & identity](race-gender-and-identity.md) — and it polices itself through
[deviance & social control](deviance-and-social-control.md). It changes through
[social movements](social-movements-and-collective-behavior.md), runs on
[social networks & capital](social-networks-and-capital.md), is co-shaped by
[technology](technology-and-society.md), and is studied through
[sociological methods](sociological-methods.md).
