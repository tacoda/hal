# Eastern Philosophy

The great philosophical traditions of Asia — Indian, Chinese, and Japanese — treated as
philosophy, not only religion. Where the [Western tradition](../philosophy/index.md) foregrounds
metaphysics and epistemology pursued by argument, the Eastern traditions tend to fuse philosophy
with a **way of life**: the aim is not only to know the truth but to be transformed by it —
liberation (*moksha*, *nirvana*) or sagehood (the Confucian *junzi*, the Daoist sage). This folder
is the college-level core, and it shares deep borders with [religion](../religion/index.md) (many
of these are also living religions), [ethics](../philosophy/ethics.md), and
[philosophy of mind](../philosophy/philosophy-of-mind.md) (the self, consciousness, non-self).

## The shape of the field

[Eastern philosophy](what-is-eastern-philosophy.md) divides into two great streams. The **Indian**
stream runs from [Hindu philosophy](hindu-philosophy.md) and its shared framework of
[karma, samsara, and moksha](karma-samsara-and-moksha.md) through the heterodox revolts of
[Buddhism](buddhism.md) — later branching into [many schools](buddhist-schools.md) — and
[Jainism](jainism.md). The **Chinese** stream runs from the Hundred Schools:
[Confucianism](confucianism.md), [Daoism](daoism.md), and
[Mohism and Legalism](mohism-and-legalism.md), all resting on a correlative cosmology of
[yin-yang and qi](yin-yang-and-chinese-cosmology.md), and reworked centuries later by
[Neo-Confucianism](neo-confucianism.md). These met and transformed again in Japan as
[Zen and Japanese philosophy](zen-and-japanese-philosophy.md). The folder closes by setting
[Eastern and Western philosophy side by side](eastern-and-western-philosophy-compared.md).

## Concepts

- [What Is Eastern Philosophy](what-is-eastern-philosophy.md) — scope, the traditions, orthodox vs heterodox, philosophy as self-transformation
- [Hindu Philosophy](hindu-philosophy.md) — the six darshanas, Vedanta, Brahman/Atman, maya
- [Karma, Samsara, and Moksha](karma-samsara-and-moksha.md) — the shared Indian framework of action, rebirth, and liberation
- [Buddhism](buddhism.md) — the Four Noble Truths, the Eightfold Path, dukkha, anatta, dependent origination, nirvana
- [Buddhist Schools](buddhist-schools.md) — Theravada, Mahayana, Madhyamaka (emptiness), Yogacara, Vajrayana, Chan/Zen
- [Jainism](jainism.md) — ahimsa, anekantavada (many-sidedness), karma as matter, the ascetic path
- [Confucianism](confucianism.md) — ren, li, the junzi, filial piety, rectification of names
- [Daoism](daoism.md) — the Dao, wu wei, ziran, the sage; Laozi and Zhuangzi
- [Mohism and Legalism](mohism-and-legalism.md) — impartial care vs. the Legalist state; the other Hundred Schools
- [Yin-Yang and Chinese Cosmology](yin-yang-and-chinese-cosmology.md) — yin-yang, the five phases, qi, the I Ching
- [Neo-Confucianism](neo-confucianism.md) — Zhu Xi and Wang Yangming; li and qi, principle and mind
- [Zen and Japanese Philosophy](zen-and-japanese-philosophy.md) — Chan→Zen, koan and satori, Shinto, Bushido, the Kyoto School, aesthetics
- [Eastern and Western Philosophy Compared](eastern-and-western-philosophy-compared.md) — self vs non-self, argument vs cultivation, being vs becoming

## Canonical works

- [The Upanishads](the-upanishads.md) — the philosophical core of the Vedas; Brahman and Atman
- [The Bhagavad Gita](the-bhagavad-gita.md) — duty, action, and yoga; the most-read Hindu text
- [The Dhammapada](the-dhammapada.md) — the Buddha's path in verse
- [The Dao De Jing](the-dao-de-jing.md) — Laozi; the Way and its power
- [The Zhuangzi](the-zhuangzi.md) — Daoist skepticism, spontaneity, and the relativity of views
- [The Analects](the-analects.md) — Confucius on virtue, ritual, and governance
