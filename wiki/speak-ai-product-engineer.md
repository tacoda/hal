# AI Product Engineer @ Speak (job posting)

A real job posting cited as evidence that the literal title **"AI Product
Engineer"** now exists at AI companies — see [The AI Product Engineer](ai-product-engineer.md).
Speak builds an LLM-powered language tutor (mobile, conversation-first); the role
sits on the Machine Learning team but is framed as **full-stack product
engineering over LLMs**, not ML research.

## What the role actually is

*"Developing innovative product experiences powered by LLMs and deploying them to
millions of users"* — tasks **span the full stack and beyond**:

- Build & deploy LLM-powered features across the stack; improve existing
  AI-powered features (conversational onboarding, lessons, grammatical
  assessment/feedback, personalized lessons).
- Collaborate cross-functionally with Engineering, Applied ML, Product, Design,
  Content.
- Refine the **process** for building LLM apps: prompting best practices,
  experimentation/evaluation, **LLM Ops**, measuring quality & performance.
- Scale features to more users and languages.

## What they look for

- 3–5+ years **full-stack/backend, product-focused** software engineering.
- React / Node / TypeScript **and** Python.
- **Real-world experience shipping LLM apps** — earned intuition for what works.
- Intuition for improving LLM output **quality and performance**.
- **LLM Ops** tooling: vector databases, RAG, prompt ops.
- Strong **product intuition** — think cross-functionally about new LLM-powered
  capabilities.
- Works independently at high velocity.

Comp (posted): **$150K–$280K + equity**, San Francisco, hybrid/remote.

## Why it's in HAL

The requirements map almost exactly onto the
[agentic coding vs AI engineering](agentic-coding-vs-ai-engineering.md)
distinction: this is an **AI-engineering / integrator** role (wire models, RAG,
tools, evals into a product) fused with **product ownership** — not model
training. It's the concrete hiring-side instance of the
[AI product engineer](ai-product-engineer.md) pattern: own the outcome, ship LLM
features end-to-end, care about evals and LLM Ops.
