# Build or Buy AI Agents (Marr)

Bernard Marr's Forbes framing: for most organizations the question is no longer
*whether* to use AI agents but *how to deploy* them — and **build-vs-buy is a
strategic choice, not a technical detail.** It shapes cost, flexibility, data
control, compliance risk, skills demand, and your ability to differentiate for
years.

## The trade-off

An off-the-shelf agentic platform gives faster time-to-value, lower maintenance,
and **inherited compliance** — the vendor carries much of the security and data-
protection burden. Building your own maximizes fit, control, and differentiation,
but you then own **"all aspects of compliance and data protection yourself."**
Choose wrong and you're either locked into tools that cap innovation or drowning in
complexity you weren't prepared to manage.

Marr's guidance is to reason from the **problems you're solving, the data involved,
and your organization's capabilities** — not from a default preference. This is the
[build vs buy](build-vs-buy.md) decision applied specifically to autonomous agents.

## Why it matters

The sharpest point (developed further by others in the debate): cheap to *build* is
not cheap to *own*, and the ownership burden lands hardest on the parts that touch
sensitive data and regulators. Buy when the capability is a vendor's core
competency and lock-in doesn't threaten differentiation; build when the
differentiation lives in the workflow or the data control is non-negotiable. See
[build vs buy](build-vs-buy.md) for the synthesized decision rule and the
complementary "build, build, build" and "split the stack" arguments.
