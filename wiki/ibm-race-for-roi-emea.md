# IBM Race for ROI — EMEA Productivity Study

IBM's *"The Race for ROI"* (with Censuswide), surveying **3,500 senior executives
across ten EMEA countries**. The headline: **66%** say AI has already driven
significant operational productivity gains, and **92%** expect agentic AI to
deliver measurable ROI within two years.

## Reported numbers

- **66%** report significant operational productivity improvements already.
- **~1 in 5** say they've already hit ROI goals; a further **~42%** expect ROI
  within 12 months across cost reduction (41%), time savings (45%), revenue (37%),
  employee satisfaction (42%), and NPS (43%).
- **92%** expect agentic AI to deliver measurable ROI within two years.
- Uneven distribution: **72% of large enterprises** (1,001–5,000 staff) report
  gains vs **55% of SMEs**; public sector lags private.
- Biggest-gain areas: **software development & IT (32%)**, customer service (32%),
  procurement (27%).
- **~85%** rate interoperability, transparency, and choice of AI systems as
  important — enterprises want autonomy and no lock-in.
- Top barriers to scaling pilots: **trustworthiness of AI (68%)** and **IT
  complexity / legacy integration (68%)**.

IBM's five leader priorities: an effective **operating model** for AI (federated or
hub-and-spoke, clear ownership); **AI literacy** board-to-entry; comfort with
**uncertainty and rapid change**; understanding **risk**; and prioritizing **open,
interoperable** systems.

## The critical caveat

This is **self-reported executive sentiment, not measured delivery.** As
[calculating ROI](calculating-roi.md) stresses, the gap between *perceived* and
*measured* gains is the core trap — surveys amplify optimism bias, so these figures
should be read as **mood, not evidence.** A leader claiming "significant
productivity gains" is not the same as a controlled measurement of shipped outcomes
(contrast the control-group discipline in
[measuring ROI of code assistants](measuring-roi-of-code-assistants.md), and the
org-level evaporation in [the AI productivity paradox](ai-productivity-paradox.md)).
The 92% agentic-ROI expectation is a forecast about a future that hasn't been
measured yet.
