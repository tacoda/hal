# Logic

The study of valid inference — what follows from what. This folder spans **formal logic**
(propositional and predicate logic, their proof theory and semantics), its **formal
extensions** (Boolean algebra, modal and non-classical logics), and **informal logic**
(argumentation and fallacies in natural language). It is the shared root of
[mathematics](../math/index.md) (proof), [computer science](../computer-science/index.md)
(circuits, computability), and [AI](../ai/knowledge-representation-and-reasoning.md)
(symbolic reasoning). *The epistemological/philosophical side of argument — knowledge,
justification, truth — is covered in the [philosophy](../philosophy/index.md) field
(pending).*

## The shape of the field

[Propositional logic](propositional-logic.md) reasons over whole statements;
[predicate logic](predicate-logic.md) adds quantifiers and is the workhorse of mathematics.
A logic has two sides that must agree: **syntax** — [formal systems and proof theory](formal-systems-and-proof-theory.md)
(axioms, inference, ⊢) — and **semantics** — [model theory](model-theory.md) (truth in a
structure, ⊨); soundness and completeness are the bridge, and
[Gödel's incompleteness](formal-systems-and-proof-theory.md) marks its limit.
[Boolean algebra](boolean-algebra.md) is logic as algebra, the bridge to digital circuits.
Beyond classical logic sit [modal logic](modal-logic.md) (necessity/possibility) and other
[non-classical logics](non-classical-logic.md) (intuitionistic, fuzzy, paraconsistent). The
logic/computation boundary is [computability and decidability](computability-and-decidability.md).
On the natural-language side, [informal logic and argumentation](informal-logic-and-argumentation.md)
judges everyday reasoning, and [fallacies](fallacies.md) catalogs how it fails.

## Concepts

- [Propositional Logic](propositional-logic.md) — connectives, truth tables, equivalence, entailment, normal forms
- [Predicate Logic](predicate-logic.md) — first-order logic: predicates, quantifiers, interpretations, satisfaction
- [Boolean Algebra](boolean-algebra.md) — the algebra of true/false, duality, Boolean functions; the bridge to circuits
- [Formal Systems and Proof Theory](formal-systems-and-proof-theory.md) — axioms, inference, ⊢ vs ⊨, soundness/completeness, Gödel
- [Model Theory](model-theory.md) — structures, satisfaction, semantic consequence, compactness, Löwenheim–Skolem
- [Modal Logic](modal-logic.md) — necessity/possibility, Kripke possible worlds; alethic/temporal/epistemic/deontic
- [Non-Classical Logic](non-classical-logic.md) — intuitionistic (Curry–Howard), many-valued/fuzzy, paraconsistent, relevance
- [Computability and Decidability](computability-and-decidability.md) — Church–Turing, the halting problem, incompleteness as undecidability
- [Informal Logic and Argumentation](informal-logic-and-argumentation.md) — premises→conclusion, validity vs soundness, Toulmin, charity
- [Fallacies](fallacies.md) — formal vs informal fallacies, why they persuade, how to spot them

## Canonical works

- [A Concise Introduction to Logic](hurley-concise-introduction-to-logic.md) — Patrick Hurley; the standard undergraduate survey (informal + formal)
- [A Mathematical Introduction to Logic](enderton-mathematical-introduction-to-logic.md) — Herbert Enderton; the mathematical-logic standard
- [An Investigation of the Laws of Thought](boole-laws-of-thought.md) — George Boole; the founding work of algebraic logic
- [An Introduction to Non-Classical Logic](priest-non-classical-logic.md) — Graham Priest; modal/intuitionistic/many-valued/paraconsistent
- [Predicate Calculus and Program Semantics](predicate-calculus-and-program-semantics.md) — Dijkstra & Scholten; weakest preconditions
- [Categorical Logic and Type Theory](categorical-logic-and-type-theory.md) — Bart Jacobs; the categorical Curry–Howard–Lambek
