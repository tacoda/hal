# Anthropology

The holistic study of humanity — across all societies and all of time, through culture,
biology, language, and material remains. This folder is the college-level core. It is the
close cousin of [sociology](../sociology/index.md) (society) and shares borders with
[linguistics](../linguistics/index.md) (language), [biology](../biology/index.md) (human
evolution), [economics](../economics/index.md) (exchange), and [religion](../religion/index.md)
(ritual & belief — pending).

## The shape of the field

[Anthropology](what-is-anthropology.md) is built on four fields and one central idea —
[the culture concept](the-culture-concept.md) — reached through its signature method,
[ethnography and fieldwork](ethnography-and-fieldwork.md), and shaped by a century of
[anthropological theory](anthropological-theory.md). The classic subjects:
[kinship and social organization](kinship-and-social-organization.md),
[human evolution & biological anthropology](human-evolution-and-biological-anthropology.md),
[linguistic anthropology](linguistic-anthropology.md), and
[archaeology & material culture](archaeology-and-material-culture.md). And the cross-cutting
domains: [ritual, symbolism & religion](ritual-symbolism-and-religion.md),
[economic anthropology](economic-anthropology.md),
[political & legal anthropology](political-and-legal-anthropology.md), and
[globalization & applied anthropology](globalization-and-applied-anthropology.md).
