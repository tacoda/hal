# Philosophy

The systematic examination of the most general questions — what exists, what we can know,
what we ought to do, and what the mind is. This folder is the college-level core, and it
sits under much of HAL: [philosophy of mind](philosophy-of-mind.md) and
[philosophy of AI](philosophy-of-ai.md) frame what [neuroscience](../neuroscience/index.md)
and [large language models](../ai/large-language-models.md) are really doing;
[ethics](ethics.md) grounds [AI governance](../ai-governance/index.md);
[philosophy of science](philosophy-of-science.md) grounds how we trust
[evals and statistics](../statistics/index.md); and the formal side of reasoning lives in
[logic](../logic/index.md).

## The shape of the field

The classical branches: [metaphysics](metaphysics.md) (what is there?),
[epistemology](epistemology.md) (what can we know?), [ethics](ethics.md) (how should we
act?), and [aesthetics](aesthetics.md) (what is beautiful?), with
[political philosophy](political-philosophy.md) on justice and authority. Reasoning itself
is examined in [critical thinking and informal logic](critical-thinking-and-informal-logic.md)
(the natural-language, fallacy-spotting companion to the formal [logic](../logic/index.md)
field). The philosophy *of* particular domains runs deep here:
[philosophy of mind](philosophy-of-mind.md) (consciousness, the mind-body problem),
[philosophy of language](philosophy-of-language.md) (meaning and reference),
[philosophy of science](philosophy-of-science.md) (paradigms, falsification), and
[philosophy of AI](philosophy-of-ai.md) (can machines think?). Perennial problems —
[free will and determinism](free-will-and-determinism.md) — and philosophy as a way of life —
[existentialism and meaning](existentialism-and-meaning.md),
[Stoicism](stoicism-and-practical-philosophy.md) — round it out.

## Concepts

- [Epistemology](epistemology.md) — knowledge, justification, truth; JTB & Gettier, rationalism vs empiricism, skepticism, Bayesian epistemology
- [Metaphysics](metaphysics.md) — being, universals, causation, identity, modality, time; the realism debate
- [Ethics](ethics.md) — meta-ethics + the three frameworks (consequentialism, deontology, virtue); the tie to AI alignment
- [Philosophy of Mind](philosophy-of-mind.md) — the mind-body problem, functionalism, qualia, the hard problem of consciousness
- [Philosophy of Science](philosophy-of-science.md) — demarcation, induction, Popper's falsification, Kuhn's paradigms, realism vs anti-realism
- [Philosophy of Language](philosophy-of-language.md) — sense & reference, meaning-as-use, speech acts; do LLMs understand?
- [Philosophy of AI](philosophy-of-ai.md) — the Turing test, Searle's Chinese Room, symbol grounding, strong vs weak AI, machine consciousness
- [Political Philosophy](political-philosophy.md) — the social contract, liberty, justice (Rawls/Nozick), rights, legitimacy
- [Free Will and Determinism](free-will-and-determinism.md) — determinism, libertarianism, compatibilism; moral responsibility; Libet
- [Aesthetics](aesthetics.md) — the philosophy of art and beauty; taste (Hume, Kant), representation, aesthetic value
- [Existentialism and Meaning](existentialism-and-meaning.md) — existence precedes essence, freedom, authenticity, the absurd
- [Stoicism and Practical Philosophy](stoicism-and-practical-philosophy.md) — philosophy as a way of life; the dichotomy of control; the modern revival
- [Critical Thinking and Informal Logic](critical-thinking-and-informal-logic.md) — evaluating natural-language arguments; validity vs strength, charity, and the fallacy catalog

## Canonical works

- [Republic](plato-republic.md) — Plato; justice, the Forms, the cave
- [Nicomachean Ethics](aristotle-nicomachean-ethics.md) — Aristotle; virtue ethics, eudaimonia, the golden mean
- [Meditations on First Philosophy](descartes-meditations-on-first-philosophy.md) — Descartes; methodic doubt, the cogito, dualism
- [Critique of Pure Reason](kant-critique-of-pure-reason.md) — Kant; the synthetic a priori, phenomena vs noumena
- [The Structure of Scientific Revolutions](kuhn-structure-of-scientific-revolutions.md) — Kuhn; paradigms and revolutions
- [Computing Machinery and Intelligence](turing-computing-machinery-and-intelligence.md) — Turing; the imitation game — can machines think?
