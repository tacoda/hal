# The Rise of the Forward Deployed Engineer

A whiteboard argument for why the most valuable AI engineers no longer sit behind
tickets: the **Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE)** sits between the traditional software
engineer and the consultant/solutions architect.

- **Traditional SWE:** builds product, ships features, works internally.
- **Consultant / solutions architect:** advises clients, creates slides, defines
  requirements.
- **FDE (the bridge):** embedded with the customer, writes production code, integrates
  AI into workflows, solves real operational problems, owns deployment to production,
  bridges product + customer + execution.

The FDE is the bridge between AI models, enterprise systems, real operational workflows,
and customer reality.

**Why the role is exploding:** (1) AI is no longer a demo problem, it's an integration
problem; (2) enterprise AI fails at deployment, not at prompting; (3) the hardest part is
making AI work inside messy systems; (4) FDEs turn prototypes into operational
capability; (5) every major AI company is hiring them (Palantir, OpenAI, Anthropic).

**The new AI stack:** model → context → tools → workflows → **deployment** → business
outcome. "Make the software work in reality, not in theory."
