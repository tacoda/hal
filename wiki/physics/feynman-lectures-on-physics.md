# The Feynman Lectures on Physics

A three-volume set (Feynman, Leighton, and Sands) built from the introductory
course Richard Feynman taught at Caltech in the early 1960s. It is less a
problem-set textbook than a sustained argument for *understanding* physics —
Feynman consistently starts from the phenomenon and the physical picture, then
lets the mathematics fall out, rather than the reverse. The full text is now
freely readable online in a high-quality HTML edition hosted by Caltech.

## Scope and structure

The three volumes track the standard arc of a physics education but with an
intuition-first treatment throughout:

- **Volume I — mechanics, radiation, and heat.** Kinematics and Newton's laws,
  conservation of energy and momentum, oscillations and resonance, and an early
  detour into the kinetic theory of gases and statistical ideas. Feynman famously
  opens with the atomic hypothesis as the single most important fact in physics.
  Anchors [classical-mechanics.md](classical-mechanics.md) and touches
  [thermodynamics.md](thermodynamics.md).
- **Volume II — electromagnetism and matter.** A field-theoretic development of
  electrostatics, magnetostatics, and the full Maxwell equations, using vector
  calculus (div, grad, curl) as the working language, plus the electrodynamics of
  matter. Anchors [electromagnetism.md](electromagnetism.md).
- **Volume III — quantum mechanics.** A distinctive amplitude-first presentation
  built around the two-slit experiment and the behavior of quantum amplitudes,
  the Stern-Gerlach experiment, and spin, rather than the historical
  Schrödinger-equation-first route. Anchors
  [quantum-mechanics.md](quantum-mechanics.md).

## Why it matters

The Lectures are prized for the *why* behind the formalism: dimensional
reasoning, orders of magnitude, symmetry arguments, and the habit of checking a
result against physical intuition. They lean on calculus and vector calculus
throughout — see [../math/calculus.md](../math/calculus.md) and
[../math/multivariable-calculus.md](../math/multivariable-calculus.md) — but the
mathematics is always in service of a picture. As a first read they can be
demanding (the exercises live in a separate *Tips* supplement); they shine most
as a companion that repairs and deepens understanding built elsewhere.
