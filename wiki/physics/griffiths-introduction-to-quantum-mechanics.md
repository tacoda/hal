# Introduction to Quantum Mechanics (Griffiths)

David J. Griffiths' standard undergraduate quantum mechanics text (later editions
co-authored with Darrell Schroeter). It is the most widely used first course in
QM, valued for the same clear, informal voice as his electrodynamics book. It
takes a deliberately pragmatic stance: teach students to *use* quantum mechanics
first, and defer the interpretive puzzles to a later, self-contained discussion.
Now published by Cambridge University Press.

## Scope and approach

The book opens directly with the Schrödinger equation and the statistical
interpretation of the wavefunction, then works through the machinery:

- **The wavefunction** — probability, normalization, momentum, and the
  uncertainty principle.
- **Time-independent Schrödinger equation** — the infinite square well, the
  harmonic oscillator (via both power series and ladder operators), the free
  particle, and the delta-function and finite-well potentials.
- **Formalism** — Hilbert space, observables as Hermitian operators, eigenvalues,
  Dirac notation, and the generalized statistical interpretation. This part rests
  squarely on [../math/linear-algebra.md](../math/linear-algebra.md).
- **Quantum mechanics in three dimensions** — the hydrogen atom, angular
  momentum, and spin.
- **Identical particles** and multi-particle systems.
- **Applications and approximation methods** — time-independent and time-
  dependent perturbation theory, the variational principle, the WKB
  approximation, and scattering.
- **The adiabatic approximation and afterword** on interpretation (measurement,
  the EPR paradox, Bell's theorem).

## Why it matters

Its pedagogical bet is that a linear-algebra-forward, calculation-focused route
gives students working command of QM before the conceptual debates. It is the
primary anchor for [quantum-mechanics.md](quantum-mechanics.md) and depends
heavily on both [../math/linear-algebra.md](../math/linear-algebra.md) (state
vectors, operators, eigenproblems) and
[../math/differential-equations.md](../math/differential-equations.md) (solving
the Schrödinger equation).
