# Classical Mechanics (John R. Taylor)

John R. Taylor's *Classical Mechanics* is the standard intermediate mechanics
text, sitting between the Newtonian mechanics of a first-year course and graduate
treatments like Goldstein. Published by University Science Books, it is widely
praised for exceptionally clear exposition and a large, well-graded problem set,
and it serves as the bridge into the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulations of
mechanics.

## Scope and approach

The book starts by consolidating Newtonian mechanics, then rebuilds mechanics on
the more powerful variational and analytical foundations:

- **Newtonian foundations** — Newton's laws, projectiles with air resistance,
  momentum and angular momentum, energy, and oscillations.
- **Lagrangian mechanics** — the calculus of variations, the principle of least
  action, generalized coordinates, and the Euler-Lagrange equations, which
  reframe dynamics in terms of energy rather than force.
- **Central-force and two-body problems** — Kepler orbits and conservation laws.
- **Rigid bodies and rotating frames** — the inertia tensor, Euler's equations,
  and non-inertial effects (Coriolis, centrifugal).
- **Coupled oscillators and normal modes.**
- **Hamiltonian mechanics** — the Hamiltonian, Hamilton's equations, phase space,
  and (in the later chapters) an introduction to nonlinear dynamics and chaos,
  plus continuum/collision topics.

## Why it matters

Its central lesson is that the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulations are not
just alternative bookkeeping but a deeper, coordinate-free way to see mechanics —
one that generalizes cleanly to field theory and quantum mechanics. The
mathematical prerequisites step up from introductory physics: it relies on
[../math/multivariable-calculus.md](../math/multivariable-calculus.md) and
[../math/differential-equations.md](../math/differential-equations.md), and the
variational calculus it introduces underpins much of modern physics. It is the
primary intermediate anchor for
[classical-mechanics.md](classical-mechanics.md).
