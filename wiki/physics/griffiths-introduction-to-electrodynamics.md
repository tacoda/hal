# Introduction to Electrodynamics (Griffiths)

David J. Griffiths' standard undergraduate text on classical electromagnetism,
used in the intermediate E&M course that typically follows introductory physics.
It is known for an unusually conversational, opinionated voice that walks through
the physics and the mathematics side by side, making a notoriously formal subject
approachable without sacrificing rigor. Now published by Cambridge University
Press (earlier editions by Pearson/Prentice Hall).

## Scope and approach

The book builds Maxwell's electromagnetism from the ground up, front-loading the
vector calculus it depends on:

- **Vector analysis** — a dedicated opening chapter on gradient, divergence,
  curl, line/surface/volume integrals, and the fundamental theorems, since these
  are the language of the whole subject. Leans on
  [../math/multivariable-calculus.md](../math/multivariable-calculus.md).
- **Electrostatics** — Coulomb's law, the electric field, potential, Gauss's law,
  work and energy, and conductors.
- **Potentials and boundary-value problems** — Laplace's and Poisson's equations,
  separation of variables, multipole expansions.
- **Electric fields in matter** — polarization, bound charge, dielectrics.
- **Magnetostatics and magnetic fields in matter** — the Biot-Savart and Ampère
  laws, the vector potential, magnetization.
- **Electrodynamics** — electromotive force, Faraday's law, Maxwell's equations
  in their full glory, and conservation laws.
- **Electromagnetic waves, potentials and radiation, and relativity** —
  propagation, gauge freedom, retarded potentials, and the relativistic unity of
  the electric and magnetic fields.

## Why it matters

This is the canonical bridge between plug-and-chug intro E&M and graduate-level
treatments. Its guiding narrative is that all of electromagnetism collapses into
Maxwell's four equations plus the Lorentz force law, and everything else is
consequence. It is the primary anchor for
[electromagnetism.md](electromagnetism.md) and pairs with the differential-
equation techniques in
[../math/differential-equations.md](../math/differential-equations.md).
