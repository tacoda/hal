# Canva: Yes, You Can Use AI in Our Interviews

Canva redesigned its technical interviews so that Backend, Machine Learning, and
Frontend candidates are now **expected** to use AI tools — Copilot, Cursor, Claude —
during the interview. Not merely tolerated: *insisted upon*. The reasoning is that the
hiring process should mirror the tools engineers use every day, and "fighting this
reality" would select for a skill Canva no longer wants to measure in isolation.

The bet is that AI availability makes the interview a **better** predictor, not a
worse one. When the assistant does the rote typing, the interview surfaces the thing
that actually matters at Canva: whether a candidate can blend human creativity and
judgment with AI capabilities. Proficiency with these tools is framed as *essential*
to the day-to-day role, part of a broader "AI Everywhere" philosophy — not a niche
interview trick.

Practical adjustments:

- Candidates are **told ahead of time** they'll be expected to use AI, and are strongly
  encouraged to **practice** with the tools before the interview.
- Canva reports the AI-assisted format feels **more engaging** for both sides and gives
  **stronger predictive signal** about performance.
- The approach is explicitly provisional — "what works today may need refinement in six
  months as AI capabilities advance."

This is one of the clearest employer-side statements of the shift described in
[Hiring in the AI Era](hiring-in-the-ai-era.md): don't police AI out of the room, bring
it in, and grade the judgment that remains. It sits alongside
[Meta's AI-enabled coding interview](meta-ai-enabled-coding-interview.md) as a concrete
production example of the pattern.
