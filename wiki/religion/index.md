# Religion (Religious Studies)

The academic, comparative study of religion — what it is, how it works, and how the major
traditions believe and practice — approached neutrally and descriptively (this is religious
studies, not theology: it describes and explains, it does not advocate or adjudicate truth
claims). This folder is the college-level core. It shares deep borders with
[anthropology](../anthropology/ritual-symbolism-and-religion.md) (ritual & symbol),
[sociology](../sociology/index.md) (religion as institution), [psychology](../psychology/index.md)
(religious experience), and [philosophy](../philosophy/index.md).

## The shape of the field

It opens with the hard question — [what is religion?](what-is-religion.md) — and the
[theories](theories-of-religion.md) that try to explain it (Durkheim, Weber, Marx, Freud,
Otto, Eliade, Geertz), organized around [the sacred and the profane](the-sacred-and-the-profane.md).
The dimensions of religious life: [myth, ritual, and symbol](myth-ritual-and-symbol.md),
[scripture and sacred texts](scripture-and-sacred-texts.md),
[religious experience and mysticism](religious-experience-and-mysticism.md), and
[religion and society](religion-and-society.md). The major traditions, mapped
[comparatively](comparative-religion-and-world-traditions.md):
[Abrahamic](abrahamic-traditions.md), [Dharmic & Eastern](dharmic-and-eastern-traditions.md),
and [indigenous & folk](indigenous-and-folk-religions.md). And the modern condition:
[religion, science, and secularism](religion-science-and-secularism.md).
