# Principles of Neural Science

*Eric R. Kandel, John D. Koester, Sarah H. Mack, and Steven A. Siegelbaum (eds.); earlier
editions with James H. Schwartz and Thomas M. Jessell.* First published 1981; now in its
sixth edition (2021, McGraw-Hill), grown from a 468-page primer to a ~1,650-page
compendium. It is the field's definitive reference — the book that establishes how a
generation of neuroscientists and clinicians frame the discipline. Kandel, a Nobel
laureate (2000) for his work on the cellular mechanisms of memory in *Aplysia*, anchors
the book's central conviction: that mind is explicable in terms of the biology of the
brain, and that behavior can be traced down to molecules and up to cognition.

## Scope and approach

The book's organizing thesis is **reductionist but integrative** — it argues that
everything from perception to emotion to disease ultimately rests on the signaling
properties of individual [nerve cells](neuron.md) and the connections between them, yet it
insists on tracing the causal chain all the way up to behavior. It moves deliberately
across levels of analysis:

- **Cellular and molecular foundations.** How a [neuron](neuron.md) is built, how its
  membrane maintains and changes electrical potential, and how signals propagate. The
  membrane biophysics and ion-channel mechanisms that underlie the resting potential and
  the [action potential](action-potential.md) are developed from first principles.
- **Synaptic transmission.** How signals cross the gap between cells —
  [synaptic transmission and neurotransmission](synapse-and-neurotransmission.md), both
  electrical and chemical, receptor pharmacology, and the modulation of synaptic strength.
- **Cognition, perception, and action.** Sensory and motor systems, and the neural basis
  of higher functions.
- **Development and plasticity.** How circuits wire themselves and how experience reshapes
  them — the material Kandel is most associated with, connecting cellular change to
  [learning and memory](learning-and-memory.md). His demonstration that memory storage
  involves changes in synaptic strength gives the book a through-line from molecule to mind.
- **Disease.** Neurological and psychiatric disorders read through the same mechanistic
  lens.

The style is authoritative and clinical, richly illustrated, and updated each edition to
absorb advances in molecular biology, imaging, and systems neuroscience. It is
simultaneously a teaching text for medical and graduate students and a standing reference
for practitioners.

## Why it anchors the field

Where computational texts model the brain, *Principles* documents it: it is the empirical
backbone that concepts in this folder rest on. Its account of how neurons integrate inputs
and fire, and how synaptic strength changes with use, is the biological substrate that the
abstractions in [artificial neural networks](../ai/neural-networks.md) and
[deep learning](../ai/deep-learning.md) simplify — the artificial "neuron" is a stripped-down
caricature of the cell this book describes in full.
