# Neuroscience (Purves et al.)

*Dale Purves, George J. Augustine, David Fitzpatrick, William C. Hall, Anthony-Samuel
LaMantia, Leonard E. White, and colleagues.* First published 1997 (Sinauer Associates;
later editions from Oxford University Press). This is the standard comprehensive
undergraduate and early-graduate textbook in neuroscience — the book most students first
learn the field from. Where [Kandel's *Principles*](kandel-principles-of-neural-science.md)
is the exhaustive reference, Purves is the pedagogically-tuned survey: clear, well-paced,
and built around a coherent tour from cells to cognition.

## Scope and approach

The book is organized as a **bottom-up survey** that moves systematically across levels
while keeping each chapter self-contained enough to teach from. Its coverage falls into
broad blocks:

- **Neural signaling.** The electrical and chemical basis of communication — the
  [neuron](neuron.md), membrane potentials, the [action potential](action-potential.md),
  and [synapses and neurotransmission](synapse-and-neurotransmission.md), including the
  major transmitter systems and receptors.
- **Sensation and sensory processing.** A large, characteristic portion of the book treats
  the [sensory systems](sensory-systems.md) in depth — vision (a Purves specialty),
  audition, the somatosensory system, chemical senses, and pain — following signals from
  receptor to cortex.
- **Movement and its central control.** Motor systems, spinal circuits, basal ganglia,
  and cerebellum.
- **The changing brain.** Development, and the plasticity of
  [neural circuits](neural-circuits.md), including
  [synaptic plasticity](synaptic-plasticity.md) and its role in
  [learning and memory](learning-and-memory.md).
- **Complex brain functions.** Higher-order cognition, language, emotion, sleep, and the
  overall [organization of the brain](brain-organization.md) — its regional anatomy and the
  functional geography of the cortex.

The approach is notably **anatomical and systems-oriented**: it grounds function in
structure, uses abundant diagrams, and repeatedly ties clinical and behavioral phenomena
back to identifiable pathways and regions. This makes it the natural companion for
understanding how the brain is laid out and how sensory information flows through it.

## Relation to computational abstractions

Purves' careful treatment of sensory hierarchies — retina to visual cortex, feature
detection stacking into representation — is the biological template that inspired
[convolutional networks](../ai/deep-learning.md) and the layered feature extraction of
[neural networks](../ai/neural-networks.md). Reading the visual-system chapters alongside
the AI notes makes the borrowed-and-simplified relationship explicit.
