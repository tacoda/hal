# Systems Thinking & Complexity

How wholes behave differently from their parts. This folder covers systems theory,
cybernetics, and complexity science — the study of feedback, emergence, and the behavior
of systems too interconnected to understand one piece at a time. It is the conceptual
backbone under a lot of HAL: [loop engineering](../harness-engineering/loop-engineering.md)
is applied [feedback](feedback-loops.md) and [cybernetics](cybernetics.md); the
reconciler pattern in [distributed systems](../distributed-systems/index.md) is a control loop;
and the [emergent abilities](emergence.md) of [large language models](../ai/large-language-models.md)
are a live example of the whole exceeding the sum.

## The shape of the field

The atom is the [feedback loop](feedback-loops.md) — reinforcing or balancing, with delays
— formalized by [cybernetics](cybernetics.md). [Complex systems](complex-systems.md) are
built from many such loops; from their interactions come [emergence](emergence.md) and
[self-organization](self-organization.md). Their dynamics can be
[chaotic](chaos-and-nonlinear-dynamics.md) (deterministic yet unpredictable) and their
geometry [fractal](fractals-and-self-similarity.md) (self-similar across scale); their
structure is studied as [networks](network-science.md) and modeled as
[stocks and flows](system-dynamics.md). Systems that adapt are
[complex adaptive systems](complex-adaptive-systems.md); systems that survive shocks have
[resilience](resilience-and-robustness.md); and systems that loop back on *themselves*
produce [strange loops](self-reference-and-strange-loops.md) — Hofstadter's account of how
an "I" arises from a substrate.

## Concepts

- [Feedback Loops](feedback-loops.md) — reinforcing vs balancing loops and delays; the atom of system behavior
- [Cybernetics](cybernetics.md) — control & communication: feedback, the governor, requisite variety, first/second order
- [Complex Systems](complex-systems.md) — many nonlinearly-coupled parts, no central control; complex ≠ complicated
- [Emergence](emergence.md) — macro order from micro interaction; weak vs strong emergence
- [Self-Organization](self-organization.md) — spontaneous order with no controller: dissipative structures, stigmergy
- [Chaos and Nonlinear Dynamics](chaos-and-nonlinear-dynamics.md) — deterministic chaos, the butterfly effect, strange attractors
- [Fractals and Self-Similarity](fractals-and-self-similarity.md) — self-similarity across scale, fractal dimension, the geometry of roughness
- [Network Science](network-science.md) — systems as graphs: small-world/scale-free topology, hubs, contagion, robust-yet-fragile
- [System Dynamics](system-dynamics.md) — stocks, flows, causal-loop diagrams, delays, leverage points
- [Resilience and Robustness](resilience-and-robustness.md) — resist vs recover vs adapt; the robustness-fragility tradeoff, antifragility
- [Complex Adaptive Systems](complex-adaptive-systems.md) — adaptive agents, local rules, emergent global behavior (Holland/Santa Fe)
- [Self-Reference and Strange Loops](self-reference-and-strange-loops.md) — tangled hierarchies, Gödel, and the loop that becomes an "I"
