# Blue Ocean Strategy (Kim & Mauborgne)

W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne's *Blue Ocean Strategy* (2005) argues that the surest path
to strong, profitable growth is not to out-compete rivals in existing markets but to
**create uncontested market space** — a "blue ocean" — where competition is irrelevant.
The metaphor contrasts two arenas.

- **Red oceans** are all the industries that exist today: known market space, defined
  boundaries, accepted rules of the game. Firms fight over a fixed pool of demand, products
  commoditize, and margins bleed away — hence "red."
- **Blue oceans** are markets not yet in existence — new demand created rather than fought
  over. Because the space is uncontested, there is room for rapid, profitable growth.

## Value innovation: breaking the value–cost trade-off

The cornerstone concept is **value innovation**. Conventional strategy (Porter's generic
strategies included) treats differentiation and low cost as a trade-off: you pick one.
Blue ocean strategy insists on pursuing **both simultaneously** — driving buyer value up
while pushing cost down — by rethinking what an industry offers rather than optimizing
within it.

| | Red ocean strategy | Blue ocean strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Market | Compete in existing space | Create uncontested space |
| Competition | Beat rivals | Make rivals irrelevant |
| Demand | Exploit existing demand | Create and capture new demand |
| Value–cost | Accept the trade-off | Break the trade-off |
| Alignment | Differentiation *or* low cost | Differentiation *and* low cost |

## Tools

The authors provide analytical instruments to make the move systematic:

- The **strategy canvas** plots competing offers against the factors an industry competes
  on, revealing where everyone is clustered and where a divergent **value curve** could
  open new space.
- The **Four Actions Framework** (Eliminate–Reduce–Raise–Create) forces the strategist to
  ask which factors the industry takes for granted can be *eliminated* or *reduced* (cutting
  cost) and which should be *raised* or newly *created* (lifting value) — the operational
  route to breaking the value–cost trade-off.

Blue Ocean Strategy is a major alternative school within [business strategy](business-strategy.md):
where Porter frames [competitive advantage](competitive-advantage.md) as defensible
positioning inside an industry, Kim and Mauborgne argue advantage comes from redrawing the
industry itself. It rhymes with new-market [disruptive innovation](disruptive-innovation.md),
draws on demand-creation ideas from [economics](../economics/index.md), and describes exactly
the market-making move many [AI businesses](../ai-business/index.md) attempt.
