# Literature

The academic study of literature — how texts are made, how they mean, and how we read and
judge them. This folder is the college-level core. It borders
[linguistics](../linguistics/index.md) (language & meaning),
[philosophy](../philosophy/aesthetics.md) (aesthetics, philosophy of language, hermeneutics),
[history](../history/index.md) (literary periods), [religion](../religion/myth-ritual-and-symbol.md)
(myth), and — freshly relevant — [large language models](../ai/large-language-models.md),
which generate and manipulate the very text literature studies.

## The shape of the field

It begins with the hard question — [what is literature?](what-is-literature.md) — sorted into
[genres and forms](literary-genres-and-forms.md) (poetry, drama, prose). How texts work:
[narrative and narratology](narrative-and-narratology.md), [poetry and poetics](poetry-and-poetics.md),
and [literary devices & figurative language](literary-devices-and-figurative-language.md). How
we study them: [literary theory and criticism](literary-theory-and-criticism.md) (the lenses),
[close reading and interpretation](close-reading-and-interpretation.md) (the practice), and
[rhetoric and style](rhetoric-and-style.md). And the big picture:
[the canon and world literature](the-canon-and-world-literature.md),
[literary periods and movements](literary-periods-and-movements.md),
[myth, archetype, and the hero's journey](myth-archetype-and-the-heros-journey.md), and
[literature and society](literature-and-society.md).

## Canonical works

- [Poetics](aristotle-poetics.md) — Aristotle; the founding work of literary theory
- [Anatomy of Criticism](frye-anatomy-of-criticism.md) — Northrop Frye; archetypal criticism
- [Mimesis](auerbach-mimesis.md) — Erich Auerbach; the representation of reality in Western literature
- [Literary Theory: An Introduction](eagleton-literary-theory.md) — Terry Eagleton; the standard theory survey
- [The Hero with a Thousand Faces](campbell-hero-with-a-thousand-faces.md) — Joseph Campbell; the monomyth
- [The Western Canon](bloom-western-canon.md) — Harold Bloom; a defense of the canon and aesthetic value
