# The Reinvention of the Dev Team

A QCon London 2026 talk by **Hannah Foxwell** (independent consultant at the
intersection of platform engineering, security, and AI; founder of *AI For the
Rest of Us*). The thesis: AI has already reshaped the developer's role over the
past two years, and the team structure that used to work no longer does.

## The argument

Foxwell used to advocate for **The Balanced Team** — a Product Manager, a
Product Designer, an Engineering Manager, and 4–8 developers. It worked. What
changed her mind is a concrete observation: **she has now seen dev teams
actually reach the end of their backlog** — something she once said she'd never
see — and she's seen it more than once. When a small team armed with agents can
burn down a backlog, the balanced team's assumptions break.

She doesn't offer a proven playbook — "we are sailing these turbulent waters
together." Instead she proposes going **back to fundamentals**: refocus on
**outcomes**, and evaluate options for **evolving team composition** — what to
keep, what to throw out, and what to keep no matter what.

## The roles she surfaces

The talk is one anchor for HAL's role notes. Two roles she frames directly:

- **Product engineer** — "engineers that don't need to ask permission of a
  product manager to go and improve the product." (See
  [The AI Product Engineer](ai-product-engineer.md) and
  [Product Engineer vs Software Engineer](product-engineer-vs-software-engineer.md).)
- **Forward-deployed engineer** — a close cousin: an empowered engineer who
  "sits side by side with the users" and fixes the product directly when they
  spot a gap — the same discovery instinct from the engineering side. (See
  [Forward-Deployed Engineer](forward-deployed-engineer.md).)

These sit alongside the broader set in the
[five engineering archetypes](five-engineering-archetypes.md), and the whole
talk is an org-level view of the shift [from coder to orchestrator](from-coder-to-orchestrator.md).
