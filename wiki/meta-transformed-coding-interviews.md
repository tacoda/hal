# Meta Just Transformed Their Coding Interviews with AI

Fahim ul Haq (Educative co-founder) frames Meta's pilot for a developer audience: an
AI tool is integrated into the interview environment so candidates write real code,
invoke AI assistance, and are **evaluated on how they use it as part of their workflow.**
In short — candidates may use an AI assistant to solve the problem, and the point is no
longer whether you can produce code unaided but whether you can *direct* the assistant
well.

The core message for developers: the closed-book, from-memory coding screen is being
displaced by a format that assumes the tools you actually work with. Preparing means
practicing the AI-assisted loop — decomposing the problem, prompting at the right
granularity, reviewing and verifying what the model returns, and staying in control of
the solution rather than pasting wholesale output. The competencies being graded
(problem solving, code quality, verification, communication) are the durable ones; AI
availability just moves the mechanical typing out of the way so those show through.

This is the practitioner-facing companion to the detailed rubric in
[Meta's AI-Enabled Coding Interview](meta-ai-enabled-coding-interview.md), and another
data point for the broader shift in [Hiring in the AI Era](hiring-in-the-ai-era.md):
employers are bringing AI *into* the interview rather than policing it out.
