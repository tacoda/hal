# Principles of Agentic Development

The market conflates **shipping code** with **developing software**. LLMs already
generate syntactically correct code at scale — that problem is solved. What remains
human is *developing software*: understanding requirements, managing complexity,
ensuring reliability, and governing change. Agentic development addresses this through
**structured collaboration between humans and agents**, not by handing the whole job to
a model.

The handbook frames the discipline as ten principles:

1. **Agents build the code; humans direct the system.** The division of labor is
   explicit — see [from coder to orchestrator](from-coder-to-orchestrator.md).
2. **Context is the bottleneck, not typing speed.** Echoes [comprehension debt](comprehension-debt.md):
   understanding, not output, is what's scarce.
3. **Code becomes increasingly ephemeral; specs endure.** The durable artifact shifts
   from the implementation to the specification.
4. **Software engineering best practices expand, not disappear.** Discipline grows in
   scope rather than being automated away.
5. **Specs must be structured and machine-readable.** Agents need unambiguous inputs to
   produce reliable outputs — the basis of [spec-driven development](spec-driven-development.md).
6. **The entire SDLC is redesigned for human-agent collaboration** — the reframing in
   [AI SDLC](ai-sdlc.md).
7. **New expertise paths replace old ones.** The skills that matter shift, they don't
   vanish.
8. **Teams explicitly include both agents and humans** as first-class members.
9. **Enterprise governance is built in, not bolted on.**
10. **Vibe coding is not agentic development.** Deliberate structure is the dividing
    line — contrast [vibe coding](vibe-coding.md), which sets that structure aside.
