# Anthropic — Product Engineer, Computer Use

A job posting (San Francisco, ~$300–320k, posted mid-2026, now closed) worth
keeping as a **concrete, real-world definition of the AI product engineer
role** at a frontier lab. The Computer Use team teaches Claude to see and
operate computer interfaces, and builds the agent harness and end-user products
around that capability. The team sits *inside* Anthropic's research org and
"closes the loop between product and model" — no layers between the engineer,
the model, and the user.

## What the role is

A product engineer here **owns end-to-end delivery** of computer-use and
browser-control product surfaces, building the **full stack** — UI, agent
runtime, backend services. Priorities shift often; the posting explicitly asks
for a **high tolerance for ambiguity** and the judgment to continuously
re-prioritize toward the highest-impact work.

**Key responsibilities:**

- Own end-to-end delivery of the product surfaces: **scope, build, ship,
  measure, and iterate**.
- Diagnose and fix reliability/robustness issues in the computer-use agent
  harness that block real-world usage.
- Partner with computer-use researchers and with the Claude Cowork team on
  shared surfaces and knowledge-worker workflows.
- Instrument products and use **usage data to drive prioritization**.
- Translate **fuzzy user pain points into concrete, shippable features**.

## What they hire for

**Minimum:** shipping a product **zero-to-one with end-to-end ownership**
(founding/early engineer or equivalent); strong **full-stack** skills (real
frontend + backend); hands-on experience with **LLM APIs, prompting, or agent
frameworks**; a track record of shipping to external users and iterating on
feedback.

**Preferred:** strong product-design instincts (can produce a clean, usable
interface without a dedicated designer); browser-automation experience.

## Why it matters

This is the [AI Product Engineer](ai-product-engineer.md) profile stated as a
hiring bar: end-to-end ownership, full-stack, comfort with ambiguity, direct
contact with users and the model. It's the same "own the outcome, not just the
code" idea from the [Product Engineer Manifesto](product-engineer-manifesto.md)
and [PostHog's framing](product-engineer-vs-software-engineer.md), and it
matches the shift [from coder to orchestrator](from-coder-to-orchestrator.md) —
here the engineer works *directly* on the agent harness rather than through
handoffs. Fixing the harness so agents "get it right the first time" is exactly
the [forward-deployed engineer](forward-deployed-engineer.md) instinct applied
inside a lab.
