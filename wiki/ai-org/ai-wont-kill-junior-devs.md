# AI Won't Kill Junior Devs — But Your Hiring Strategy Might

Addy Osmani's argument: junior developers stay essential in an AI-heavy
industry, but their **role is evolving, not disappearing** — and the real threat
isn't AI, it's companies that respond by refusing to hire juniors at all.

**tl;dr:** AI now writes the boilerplate juniors used to write, so juniors must
move up to debugging, system design, and collaboration. Firms that cut junior
roles entirely gamble away their future talent pipeline. The juniors who thrive
use AI as a *learning tool, not a crutch* — verifying its output, understanding
the "why," and deliberately building the ability to read and comprehend code.

## The short-term temptation

If an AI pair-programmer can autocomplete a function or fix a trivial bug with
little human input, managers ask, as Sourcegraph CEO Quinn Slack put it:

> If AI can fix small bugs or add small features with little human intervention,
> then why pay a junior dev to do that instead?

So the bar for juniors rises: they're expected to contribute in ways AI can't —
which paradoxically requires exactly the experience the boilerplate work used to
build.

## No juniors today means no seniors tomorrow

This is the core warning, and it's the same missing-rung logic as
[ironies of automation](../systems-thinking/ironies-of-automation.md) and
[learning the craft](learning-the-craft.md). Every senior once started as a
junior:

- **Camille Fournier:** "How do people ever become 'senior engineers' if they
  don't start out as junior ones?"
- **Charity Majors** (paraphrased via Osmani): skipping junior training is
  "cannibalizing our own future."

Managers shifting to "senior-only" hiring are, in Fournier's words, asking for
trouble. The pipeline is a system: starve the input now and the supply of
seniors collapses later.

## What to do instead

Keep hiring and developing juniors, but redirect their reps toward
*understanding* rather than just shipping: reading and comprehending code,
debugging, design, and using AI as an always-on tutor while verifying what it
produces. That's the deliberate teaching that decides whether the AI era grows
strong engineers or hollow ones — the optimistic reading of
[revenge of the junior developer](revenge-of-the-junior-developer.md), made
conditional on not gutting the pipeline.
