# Political Science

The academic study of politics — power, the state, government, and collective
decision-making — approached analytically and non-partisanly (it describes and explains
positions across the spectrum, it does not advocate). This folder is the college-level core.
It shares the normative questions of [philosophy → political philosophy](../philosophy/political-philosophy.md),
the incentive analysis of [economics](../economics/index.md), and the group-level view of
[sociology](../sociology/index.md); it also frames [AI governance & regulation](../ai-governance/index.md).

## The shape of the field

At the center sit [power, authority, and legitimacy](power-authority-and-legitimacy.md) and
[the state and sovereignty](the-state-and-sovereignty.md), organized into
[forms of government](forms-of-government.md) and mapped by
[political theory and ideologies](political-theory-and-ideologies.md). The study of
democracy runs through [democracy and elections](democracy-and-elections.md),
[constitutions and the rule of law](constitutions-and-rule-of-law.md), and
[political behavior and participation](political-behavior-and-participation.md).
[Comparative politics](comparative-politics.md) compares systems across countries;
[international relations](international-relations.md) and
[geopolitics and security](geopolitics-and-security.md) study how states interact; and
[political economy](political-economy.md) and
[public policy and governance](public-policy-and-governance.md) study how states and markets
shape outcomes.

## Concepts

- [Political Theory and Ideologies](political-theory-and-ideologies.md) — the left-right map; liberalism, conservatism, socialism, libertarianism, nationalism, anarchism
- [The State and Sovereignty](the-state-and-sovereignty.md) — Weber's monopoly on force, sovereignty, nation vs state, state formation
- [Forms of Government](forms-of-government.md) — democracy, authoritarianism, monarchy…; parliamentary vs presidential, federal vs unitary
- [Power, Authority, and Legitimacy](power-authority-and-legitimacy.md) — power vs authority, Weber's three legitimacies, hard vs soft power
- [Democracy and Elections](democracy-and-elections.md) — models of democracy, electoral systems (FPTP vs PR, Duverger), representation, backsliding
- [Constitutions and the Rule of Law](constitutions-and-rule-of-law.md) — separation of powers, checks & balances, judicial review, rights
- [Comparative Politics](comparative-politics.md) — the comparative method, democratization, state capacity, why states thrive or fail
- [Political Behavior and Participation](political-behavior-and-participation.md) — voting, public opinion & polling, parties, media, polarization
- [International Relations](international-relations.md) — realism, liberalism, constructivism; anarchy, the security dilemma, institutions
- [Political Economy](political-economy.md) — states & markets, collective action (Olson), rent-seeking, welfare states, varieties of capitalism
- [Public Policy and Governance](public-policy-and-governance.md) — the policy cycle, bureaucracy, regulation, evidence-based policy, AI regulation
- [Geopolitics and Security](geopolitics-and-security.md) — geopolitical theory, strategy, deterrence, alliances, cyber/information warfare
