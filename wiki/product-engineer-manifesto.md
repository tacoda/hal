# The Product Engineer Manifesto

A short, opinionated statement of what a **Product Engineer** is, written by
Viljami Kuosmanen (`@anttiviljami`) in the style of the Agile Manifesto. Its
core claim: it is the builder's job to **understand the problem before diving
into solutions**, and to refuse to be boxed into a technical-only role. The
craft is **Product Thinking combined with strong technical execution**, working
across the design, technical, *and* business domains rather than staying in one
lane.

## The mindset

The manifesto is a list of value-pairs — the left side over the right, in the
Agile "X over Y" form:

- **Continuous delivery of working software** over promises and estimations.
- **Asking why** — understanding the customer problem — over diving straight
  into code.
- **Customer collaboration and feedback** over tickets and second-hand knowledge.
- **Teamwork and communication** over picking up tasks and working in isolation.
- **Testing the product yourself** over relying on others (especially users) to
  find issues.
- **Domain knowledge and ownership** over outsourcing strategic thinking to
  someone else.

Taken together these describe someone who takes professional pride in the
*product* shipped, not just the code written — closer to a builder who happens
to engineer than an engineer who happens to be assigned product work.

## Why it matters now

This is a **pre-AI framing** (the manifesto predates the current agentic wave),
but it lines up exactly with the role AI is now pushing engineers toward: as
writing code gets cheap, the scarce, valued skill becomes deciding *what* to
build and owning the outcome. See [The AI Product Engineer](ai-product-engineer.md)
for the modern, agent-era version of this same profile, and
[Product Engineer vs Software Engineer](product-engineer-vs-software-engineer.md)
for the contrast with the traditional role. The "understand the problem first"
instinct is the discovery muscle that [from coder to orchestrator](from-coder-to-orchestrator.md)
and the [five engineering archetypes](five-engineering-archetypes.md) both
describe as rising in value.
