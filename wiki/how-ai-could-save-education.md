# How AI Could Save (Not Destroy) Education

Sal Khan (founder/CEO of Khan Academy) argues at TED2023 that against the
prevailing fear — that AI will let students cheat and hollow out learning — AI is
actually poised to drive **the biggest positive transformation education has ever
seen**, by giving *every* student a personal tutor and *every* teacher an
assistant.

## The 2 sigma problem

The framing rests on **Benjamin Bloom's 1984 "2 sigma" study**: students given
one-on-one tutoring performed **two standard deviations** better than students in
conventional classrooms — enough to turn an average student into an exceptional
one, and a below-average student into an above-average one. The catch has always
been cost: you can't afford a human tutor for every child. Khan's bet is that AI
finally makes personal tutoring **scalable and economical** — the same
democratization move Khan Academy made with video, now personalized like a human
one-on-one.

## Khanmigo — what it does

The demos of Khan Academy's AI, **Khanmigo**, show the design principle
throughout: **it does not just hand over answers.**

- **Socratic tutoring.** Ask a math problem for the answer and it refuses —
  instead it walks the student through the reasoning, spots *where* they went
  wrong, and asks questions. Same for an American-history question: "tell me the
  answer" flips it into tutoring mode, not answer mode.
- **Reading comprehension as an oral exam.** Reading Steve Jobs's Stanford
  speech, the student clicks a prompt and the AI questions them Socratically —
  *why* did the author use that word, does it support the argument — and can
  highlight passages. A writing coach for every reader.
- **Talking to the text.** A student reading *The Great Gatsby*, puzzled by the
  green light, could "talk to Jay Gatsby himself" when web searches on the
  symbolism didn't resonate.
- **Guidance counseling at scale.** With counselor-to-student ratios often
  200–300:1, Khanmigo can act as academic/career/life coach — already live for a
  few thousand users.

## For teachers, not just students

A teacher toggle turns *student mode* off into **teacher mode** — "a teacher's
guide on steroids": it explains not just the answer but *how to teach it*,
prepares the teacher, drafts lesson plans, and (eventually) progress reports and
grading. Teachers spend roughly half their time on lesson planning; handing that
to AI returns the time to human interaction with students.

## The stance

The talk is the **optimistic counterpoint** to the cognitive-offloading worry in
[does AI make us stupid](does-ai-make-us-stupid.md) and the missing-rung worry in
[learning the craft](learning-the-craft.md): with the right guardrails (Socratic,
answer-withholding, reasoning-first), AI becomes an always-on tutor that
*enhances* learning rather than replacing the effort that builds it. The design
choice — refuse the answer, demand the reasoning — is exactly the discipline
[critical thinking during the age of AI](critical-thinking-during-the-age-of-ai.md)
asks of professionals.
