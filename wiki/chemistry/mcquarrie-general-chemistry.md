# General Chemistry (McQuarrie)

A rigorous, quantum-forward general-chemistry textbook by Donald A. McQuarrie,
Peter A. Rock, and Ethan B. Gallogly, published by University Science Books (4th
edition). McQuarrie was best known for his graduate-level texts in physical
chemistry and statistical mechanics, and this book carries that same
mathematical seriousness down into the first-year course — it is the more
demanding, theory-first alternative to the mainstream survey texts.

## Scope and approach

The 4th edition is a thorough revision that takes an explicit **atoms-first**
approach: rather than opening with macroscopic measurement and working inward, it
establishes the structure of the atom and the quantum basis of electronic
structure early, and then derives the periodic table, bonding, and reactivity as
consequences of that structure. The arc:

- **Atomic and quantum structure first** — the experimental origins of the
  quantum theory, the quantization of energy, atomic spectra, orbitals, and
  electron configuration are developed before descriptive chemistry, so that
  everything downstream is explained rather than asserted. See
  [atomic-structure.md](atomic-structure.md).
- **Periodicity as a derived result** — periodic trends follow directly from the
  electron configurations established in the atomic chapters, reinforcing the
  logic of [the-periodic-table.md](the-periodic-table.md).
- **Bonding, geometry, and reactions** — Lewis structures, VSEPR, and
  valence-bond/molecular-orbital descriptions, then stoichiometry, solution
  chemistry, thermochemistry, equilibrium, kinetics, and electrochemistry,
  each developed with more explicit derivation than a typical introductory text.

Its pedagogical signature is **mathematical honesty**: where mainstream texts
state a result, McQuarrie tends to show where it comes from, and the problem sets
lean quantitative. This makes it especially suited to students headed toward
physical chemistry or the physical sciences, and it pairs naturally with the
deeper treatment in
[Atkins' Physical Chemistry](atkins-physical-chemistry.md).

## Why it anchors the field

McQuarrie is the general-chemistry reference for readers who want the *why* built
in from the first chapter — the atoms-first, quantum-grounded counterpart to the
broader macroscopic-first survey of
[Chemistry: The Central Science](brown-lemay-chemistry-the-central-science.md).
It anchors the more rigorous readings of
[atomic-structure.md](atomic-structure.md) and
[the-periodic-table.md](the-periodic-table.md).
