# Chemistry

The science of matter — its structure, properties, and transformations. Chemistry is
[quantum physics](../physics/quantum-mechanics.md) applied to atoms, and it hands its
molecules up to [biology](../biology/index.md) (organic chemistry → the molecules of life).
This folder is the college-level core.

## The shape of the field

It starts with [atomic structure](atomic-structure.md), whose electron configurations
organize [the periodic table](the-periodic-table.md) and determine
[chemical bonding](chemical-bonding.md) and the
[intermolecular forces](states-of-matter-and-intermolecular-forces.md) that set bulk
properties. Quantitative chemistry runs on
[stoichiometry and the mole](stoichiometry-and-the-mole.md) and plays out in
[chemical reactions](chemical-reactions.md), governed by three questions: *will it go?*
([chemical thermodynamics](chemical-thermodynamics.md)), *how fast?*
([chemical kinetics](chemical-kinetics.md)), and *how far?*
([chemical equilibrium](chemical-equilibrium.md)). Special reaction families —
[acids and bases](acids-and-bases.md) and [redox & electrochemistry](redox-and-electrochemistry.md) —
and the vast [organic chemistry](organic-chemistry.md) of carbon complete the core.

- [Stoichiometry and the Mole](stoichiometry-and-the-mole.md) — the mole, molar mass, balancing, limiting reagents, yield
- [Chemical Reactions](chemical-reactions.md) — reaction types, the molecular picture, notation, energy changes
- [Chemical Thermodynamics](chemical-thermodynamics.md) — ΔH, ΔS, ΔG = ΔH − TΔS; spontaneity and coupling

- [Chemical Equilibrium](chemical-equilibrium.md) — dynamic equilibrium, K and Q, Le Chatelier as negative feedback
- [Acids and Bases](acids-and-bases.md) — Arrhenius/Brønsted/Lewis, pH, Ka/Kb, buffers, titration
- [Redox and Electrochemistry](redox-and-electrochemistry.md) — electron transfer, half-reactions, cells, potential, batteries
- [Organic Chemistry](organic-chemistry.md) — the chemistry of carbon: functional groups, isomers, reactions, polymers

- [Organic Chemistry](clayden-organic-chemistry.md) — Clayden et al.; the mechanism-first organic text
