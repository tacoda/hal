# Economics

How societies allocate scarce resources — and how the incentives of many self-interested
agents aggregate into prices, markets, and outcomes. This folder is the college-level core.
It ties into [AI](../ai/index.md) more than it looks: [game theory](game-theory.md) is the
math behind multi-agent RL, [GANs](../ai/generative-models.md), and auction/ad markets;
[behavioral economics](behavioral-economics.md) is applied [statistics](../statistics/index.md)
of human judgment; and [econometrics](econometrics.md) is
[causal inference](../statistics/causal-inference.md) on economic data. It shares an
optimization backbone with [linear optimization](../linear-optimization/index.md) and a
systems view with [systems thinking](../systems-thinking/index.md).

## The shape of the field

Economics starts from [scarcity and opportunity cost](opportunity-cost-and-scarcity.md) and
its core method, [marginal thinking and incentives](marginal-thinking-and-incentives.md).
[Supply and demand](supply-and-demand.md) shows how prices coordinate; **microeconomics**
([microeconomics](microeconomics.md)) studies individual agents and markets, while
**macroeconomics** ([macroeconomics](macroeconomics.md), [money and finance](money-and-finance.md),
[economic growth](economic-growth.md)) studies the whole economy. Markets don't always work —
[market failure and externalities](market-failure-and-externalities.md) — and agents aren't
always rational: [behavioral economics](behavioral-economics.md). Two engines power modern
economics and its AI overlap: [game theory](game-theory.md) (strategic interaction) and
[econometrics](econometrics.md) (measuring it), and
[information economics and network effects](information-economics-and-network-effects.md)
explains why tech markets behave differently.

## Concepts

- [Opportunity Cost and Scarcity](opportunity-cost-and-scarcity.md) — scarcity forces choice; opportunity cost, comparative advantage, the PPF
- [Marginal Thinking and Incentives](marginal-thinking-and-incentives.md) — MB = MC, sunk costs, incentives, and the limits of the rational actor
- [Supply and Demand](supply-and-demand.md) — the price mechanism: equilibrium, elasticity, surplus, price controls
- [Microeconomics](microeconomics.md) — consumer & producer theory, market structures, general equilibrium
- [Macroeconomics](macroeconomics.md) — GDP, inflation, unemployment, the business cycle, fiscal vs monetary policy
- [Money and Finance](money-and-finance.md) — money, interest, the time value of money, banking/credit, risk & return, bubbles
- [Economic Growth](economic-growth.md) — productivity, capital, the Solow model, technology as the long-run driver, institutions
- [Market Failure and Externalities](market-failure-and-externalities.md) — externalities, public goods, the commons, asymmetric information, monopoly
- [Game Theory](game-theory.md) — strategic interaction, Nash equilibrium, prisoner's dilemma, mechanism/auction design
- [Behavioral Economics](behavioral-economics.md) — bounded rationality, heuristics & biases, prospect theory, nudges
- [Econometrics](econometrics.md) — causal answers from data: endogeneity, instrumental variables, difference-in-differences
- [Information Economics and Network Effects](information-economics-and-network-effects.md) — asymmetric information, zero-marginal-cost goods, winner-take-all tech markets

## Canonical works

- [Principles of Economics](mankiw-principles-of-economics.md) — Mankiw; the standard intro (the Ten Principles)
- [Intermediate Microeconomics](varian-intermediate-microeconomics.md) — Hal Varian; the standard intermediate micro text
- [Theory of Games and Economic Behavior](von-neumann-morgenstern-theory-of-games.md) — von Neumann & Morgenstern; the founding work of game theory
- [Thinking, Fast and Slow](kahneman-thinking-fast-and-slow.md) — Daniel Kahneman; System 1/2, biases, prospect theory
- [The Wealth of Nations](smith-wealth-of-nations.md) — Adam Smith; the 1776 founding text
- [Introductory Econometrics](wooldridge-introductory-econometrics.md) — Jeffrey Wooldridge; the standard econometrics text
