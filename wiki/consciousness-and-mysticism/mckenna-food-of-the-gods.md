# Food of the Gods

Terence McKenna (1992), subtitle *The Search for the Original Tree of Knowledge: A Radical History of
Plants, Drugs, and Human Evolution*. McKenna's most substantial book, a sweeping (and deliberately
provocative) history of humanity's relationship with psychoactive plants.

## What it argues

- **A plant-history of culture.** McKenna surveys the role of psychoactive plants and fungi —
  psilocybin mushrooms, ayahuasca, cannabis, coca, and the stimulants sugar, coffee, and alcohol —
  across human societies, arguing that intoxicants have been central, not marginal, to the shaping of
  religion, culture, and consciousness. This ethnobotanical sweep is the book's strongest, most
  grounded material.
- **The "stoned ape" hypothesis.** Its most famous and most **speculative** claim: that dietary
  psilocybin (from mushrooms on the dung of grazing animals on the African savanna) catalyzed the
  rapid evolution of human cognition, language, and imagination in early hominins. This is a
  *hypothesis*, not established science — it lacks direct evidence and is not accepted by
  evolutionary biology (compare the mainstream account in [human
  evolution](../anthropology/human-evolution-and-biological-anthropology.md)) — but it is his signature
  provocation.
- **"Archaic revival" and dominator culture.** Drawing on Riane Eisler, McKenna contrasts a lost,
  psychedelic, "partnership" mode of society with a repressive "dominator" culture that fears the
  boundary-dissolving plants; he calls for an [archaic revival](mckenna-the-archaic-revival.md) — a
  return to plant-mediated, ecstatic, communal consciousness.

## Why it matters

*Food of the Gods* is the foundational text of modern psychedelic advocacy and a serious, if
uneven, work of ethnobotany braided with bold conjecture. Read the plant-history as scholarship and
the stoned-ape thesis as imaginative hypothesis. It touches
[anthropology](../anthropology/index.md), the study of [ritual and religious
experience](../religion/index.md), and the [psychology of consciousness](../psychology/index.md).
