# The Invisible Landscape

Terence McKenna and Dennis McKenna (1975; revised 1993), subtitle *Mind, Hallucinogens, and the I
Ching*. The brothers' most theoretical and demanding book, and the origin of Terence's most notorious
idea, **novelty theory** / **Timewave Zero**.

## What it argues

- **Mind, brain, and hallucinogens.** The first half develops a speculative model of shamanic
  experience, consciousness, and the effect of tryptamine hallucinogens on the brain, drawing on
  neuroscience, quantum ideas, and comparative religion. Ambitious and dense, it mixes real science
  with far-reaching conjecture.
- **Novelty theory and the Timewave.** Its lasting notoriety comes from the second half. From the
  King Wen sequence of the [I Ching](../eastern-philosophy/yin-yang-and-chinese-cosmology.md)'s 64
  hexagrams, the McKennas derived a mathematical "**Timewave**" purporting to graph the ebb and flow
  of *novelty* (complexity/connectedness) across all of history. The wave, as they fitted it to a
  start date, plunged to zero — maximal novelty, an "end of history" or transcendental singularity —
  around **December 2012**.
- **Status: not science.** Novelty theory is **unfalsifiable and empirically unsupported**; its
  mathematics has been criticized as arbitrary and its central prediction simply **did not occur**
  (nothing singular happened in December 2012). It is best understood as speculative metaphysics /
  numerology, not a scientific theory — a point worth stating plainly given how much of McKenna's
  later reputation rested on it.

## Why it matters

*The Invisible Landscape* is where McKenna's cosmology is laid out most fully, and understanding it
(and its failure) is essential to placing him: a genuinely erudite thinker who fused
[shamanism](../religion/index.md), [the I Ching](../eastern-philosophy/yin-yang-and-chinese-cosmology.md),
and the [psychedelic exploration of consciousness](../psychology/index.md) with a grand, ultimately
untenable predictive theory. Read the phenomenology seriously; treat the Timewave as a cautionary
example of pattern-seeking untethered from [falsifiability](../science/falsifiability-and-demarcation.md).
