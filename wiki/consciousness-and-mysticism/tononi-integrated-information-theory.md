# Integrated Information Theory (Phi)

**Giulio Tononi's** Integrated Information Theory (IIT), developed from ~2004 and popularized in his
book *Phi: A Voyage from the Brain to the Soul* (2012). One of the most prominent and mathematically
ambitious scientific theories of consciousness — and one of the most contested.

## The theory

- **Start from experience, not the brain.** IIT reverses the usual approach: it begins with the
  essential *properties* of any conscious experience (it exists, is structured, specific, unified,
  and definite) and asks what a physical system must be like to have them.
- **Integrated information (Φ, "phi").** Consciousness *is* **integrated information** — the degree to
  which a system's parts specify a whole that is *more than the sum of the parts* and cannot be
  decomposed without loss. Φ quantifies this irreducibility. A system is conscious to the extent it
  has high Φ; the level and *quality* of consciousness correspond to the structure of its integrated
  information.
- **Consequences.** Consciousness comes in degrees and can exist wherever there is sufficient
  integration; IIT implies a form of **panpsychism** (simple systems have tiny Φ) and predicts that a
  functionally equivalent but feed-forward or modular system (some computers) could behave like us
  yet have near-zero Φ — so IIT holds that the *architecture*, not just the function, matters for
  consciousness.

## Status and controversy

IIT is empirically active (linked to measures like the "perturbational complexity index" used to
detect consciousness in unresponsive patients) but sharply disputed. Critics argue Φ is
near-impossible to compute for real brains, that the panpsychist consequences are unfalsifiable
overreach, and in 2023 a group of scientists publicly labeled it "pseudoscience" — itself a
controversial charge. It remains a leading, live contender rather than settled science; weigh it
against [falsifiability](../science/falsifiability-and-demarcation.md).

## Why it matters

IIT is the boldest attempt to give consciousness a precise mathematical *measure* and to say which
physical systems have it, contrasting with the more standard-neuroscience
[global workspace](dehaene-consciousness-and-the-brain.md) and [predictive-processing](seth-being-you.md)
approaches. A central reference point in [neuroscience](../neuroscience/index.md) and
[philosophy of mind](../philosophy/philosophy-of-mind.md).
