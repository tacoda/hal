# The Ego Tunnel

Thomas Metzinger (2009), the accessible presentation of the **self-model theory of subjectivity** he
developed in his technical work *Being No One* (2003). Its startling core claim: **no one has ever
been or had a self** — the self is a model, not a thing.

## The argument

- **No such thing as a self.** There are no selves in the world; there are only **self-models** —
  representations the brain builds. What we take to be a substantial "I" is the content of an ongoing
  **phenomenal self-model**, a simulation the organism runs of itself.
- **The Ego Tunnel.** Conscious experience is a "tunnel": the brain constructs an internal
  low-dimensional model of reality, and we live *inside that model*, mistaking it for reality itself.
  Crucially, the model is **transparent** — we see *through* it without noticing it is a model, so the
  self and world appear simply given. The Ego Tunnel is that transparent world-and-self simulation.
- **Evidence from the edges.** Metzinger marshals altered states — lucid dreams, out-of-body
  experiences, phantom limbs, rubber-hand illusions, meditation — to show the self-model is
  manipulable, breakable, and constructed, not a fixed given.

## Why it matters

Metzinger gives the **no-self** view its most rigorous modern, naturalistic formulation: selfhood is
a representational process, and there is no inner entity behind it. It converges from neuroscience on
the [Buddhist doctrine of anatta](../eastern-philosophy/buddhism.md) and the
[self/non-self debate](../eastern-philosophy/eastern-and-western-philosophy-compared.md), and lines up
with [Seth's predictive self](seth-being-you.md) and [Hofstadter's strange
loop](hofstadter-i-am-a-strange-loop.md) — a striking East–West convergence in
[philosophy of mind](../philosophy/philosophy-of-mind.md). (No dedicated page per work; see the author
entry.)
