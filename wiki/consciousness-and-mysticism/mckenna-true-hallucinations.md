# True Hallucinations

Terence McKenna (1993), subtitle *Being an Account of the Author's Extraordinary Adventures in the
Devil's Paradise*. A memoir rather than a treatise — McKenna's first-person narrative of the 1971
expedition to the Colombian Amazon that he and his brother Dennis called the **Experiment at La
Chorrera**, the seedbed of nearly all his later ideas.

## What it recounts

- **The Experiment at La Chorrera.** In the rainforest near La Chorrera, the McKenna brothers,
  immersed in psilocybin mushrooms and ayahuasca, undertook a self-devised "experiment" they believed
  might bond hallucinogenic compounds with their own neural/genetic machinery to produce a lasting
  transformation of consciousness. McKenna narrates the resulting extended altered states with
  candor.
- **Genesis of the later theories.** Out of this episode came the intuitions McKenna would spend his
  life elaborating: the sense of an approaching "concrescence" or transcendental object at the end of
  time, and the [novelty theory / Timewave Zero](mckenna-the-invisible-landscape.md) later worked out
  in *The Invisible Landscape*. The book shows the *experiential* origin of ideas he elsewhere
  presented as theory.
- **Honesty about ambiguity.** McKenna is notably self-aware, wrestling openly with whether the
  experiences were revelation, delusion, or something in between — the title's paradox, "true
  hallucinations," names exactly this uncertainty.

## Why it matters

*True Hallucinations* is the personal, human core of McKenna's corpus and the key to reading the rest:
his cosmology grew from a specific, extreme psychedelic episode, not from dispassionate theorizing.
As a document of the [phenomenology of altered states](../psychology/index.md) it is vivid; its
metaphysical conclusions remain, by his own framing, unverifiable. Companion to
[The Archaic Revival](mckenna-the-archaic-revival.md) and [Food of the
Gods](mckenna-food-of-the-gods.md).
